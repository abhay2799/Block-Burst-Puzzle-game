Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 15, 2026

Devlance Studio ("we", "our", "us") built Block Burst: Puzzle Blast Game as a free, ad-supported game. This page informs users about our policies regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of information.

Information We Collect

Block Burst does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. We do not require account creation, login, or any personal data to use the app.

All game data (high scores, saved game state, preferences) is stored locally on your device only and is never sent to any server.

Third-Party Services – Advertising

Our app uses Google AdMob to display advertisements. AdMob may collect and use certain data to serve ads, including:

  • Advertising identifiers (Advertising ID)
  • General device information (device model, OS version)
  • General location data (IP-based, not precise GPS)

For more information on how Google uses data, please review Google's Privacy Policy and how Google uses information from apps.

Children's Privacy (COPPA Compliance)

Block Burst is designed to be suitable for all ages. We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and similar regulations:

  • We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13
  • Ads shown to users are configured with child-directed treatment enabled
  • No behavioral advertising is served to children
  • No user tracking or profiling is performed by our app

Data Storage

All game-related data is stored exclusively on the user's device using local storage. This includes:

  • High score
  • Current game state (for resume functionality)
  • Tutorial completion status
  • Sound preferences

This data can be cleared at any time by clearing the app's data or uninstalling the app.

Permissions

Block Burst requires internet access solely for the purpose of loading and displaying advertisements. No other device permissions are required.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected on this page with an updated revision date. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
